# Toga study intelligence

## Overview

This report summarizes the analysis performed on TOGA study activity data to understand cadet learning behavior, subject readiness, academic weak areas, and inactivity patterns. The analysis also compares study progress with flight progress to identify cadets who may require additional academic support or instructor intervention.

---

# Subject wise progress analysis

Study progress varied significantly across different cadets and subjects.

Several cadets showed strong completion percentages in individual subjects:
- C060 achieved 100% progress in Navigation
- C040 showed 92.30% average study progress
- C036 showed 91.13% study progress

At the same time, some cadets showed very low subject completion levels:
- C024 → 6.25% in Meteorology
- C010 → 7.14% in Meteorology
- C007 → 9.37% in Technical General

The analysis indicates that subject completion consistency is uneven across the training batches.

---

# Weak subject observations

Navigation and Meteorology appeared frequently in the weak subject category.

Common issues observed:
- low quiz scores despite moderate progress
- low chapter completion percentages
- incomplete subject coverage

Examples:
- C002 scored 35 in Meteorology despite moderate progress
- C003 showed only 21.42% completion in Technical General
- C041 showed weak performance across multiple subjects

Some cadets also showed low progress even when quiz scores were relatively high, which may indicate incomplete syllabus coverage rather than understanding issues alone.

---

# Quiz performance analysis

Average quiz scores across subjects were generally moderate.

| subject | average quiz score |
|----------|-------------------|
| Instrument Procedures | 73.47 |
| Meteorology | 68.09 |
| Technical General | 63.75 |
| Navigation | 61.13 |
| Air Regulations | 59.90 |

Instrument Procedures recorded the strongest overall quiz performance, while Air Regulations showed the lowest average score.

---

# Study inactivity risk

A large number of cadets showed inactivity periods greater than 30 days.

Some cadets with repeated inactivity:
- C001
- C021
- C035
- C049
- C060

Long inactivity periods may affect:
- subject retention
- quiz performance
- exam readiness
- training continuity

Meteorology and Navigation subjects appeared frequently among inactive records.

---

# Practice test readiness

Practice test readiness was evaluated using the number of practice tests attempted.

Cadets with higher practice activity generally showed stronger overall readiness scores.

Several cadets still remained in the low readiness category because of limited practice test participation despite reasonable study progress.

This suggests that chapter completion alone may not fully reflect exam preparation readiness.

---

# Relationship between study and flight progress

The comparison between study progress and flight progress showed mixed learning patterns.

Some cadets maintained balanced operational and academic progress:
- C019
- C040
- C055

However, several cadets showed strong flight progress but weak academic progress.

Examples:
- C016 → 68.88% flight progress but only 10% study progress
- C037 → 84.22% flight progress but 27.58% study progress
- C045 → 86.88% flight progress but 14.81% study progress

This may create future academic bottlenecks even when operational training progresses normally.

The opposite pattern was also observed for some cadets:
- strong study performance but weaker flight progression

This suggests imbalance between academic and operational scheduling.

---

# Study readiness score analysis

The study readiness score combined:
- subject progress
- quiz performance
- practice test activity

Higher readiness scores were observed for cadets with:
- consistent chapter completion
- strong quiz performance
- regular practice testing

Cadets with lower readiness scores generally showed:
- inactivity periods
- weaker quiz performance
- incomplete subject progression

---

# Suggested study actions

The generated recommendations mainly included:
- revise weak concepts
- complete pending chapters
- resume regular study sessions

A large portion of cadets with lower quiz scores were advised to revise weak concepts, especially in:
- Navigation
- Meteorology
- Air Regulations

Cadets with lower progress percentages were recommended to focus on chapter completion before attempting additional assessments.

---

# Instructor intervention requirement

Many cadets were marked under instructor intervention requirement because of:
- low quiz scores
- long inactivity periods
- weak subject progress

Examples requiring attention:
- C002 → weak Meteorology performance with long inactivity
- C024 → extremely low Meteorology progress
- C041 → weak performance across multiple subjects

Example operational observation:

C002 has moderate Meteorology progress but low quiz performance and has remained inactive for more than 50 days. Additional instructor follow-up and targeted revision support may help improve subject readiness.

---

# Conclusion

The TOGA learning analysis identified noticeable variation in cadet academic engagement and subject readiness.

The major academic risk factors observed were:
- prolonged inactivity
- low quiz scores
- incomplete subject progression
- imbalance between study and flight training

Navigation, Meteorology, and Air Regulations appeared repeatedly in weak subject analysis, indicating that these subjects may require closer academic monitoring.

The analysis also suggests that regular study tracking and early instructor intervention could help reduce future academic delays and improve overall cadet readiness.
