# Training progress analysis

## Overview

This report summarizes the cadet training progress analysis performed using cadet records, sortie activity, TOGA study performance, and payment information. The objective of this analysis was to identify cadets with slower training progression, operational disruptions, and potential completion risks.

---

# Cadet progress analysis

Cadet progress percentages showed a wide variation across different training programs.

Some cadets were close to completion:
- C057 → 88.60%
- C045 → 86.88%
- C019 → 84.44%
- C040 → 83.87%

At the same time, several cadets were still below 20% completion:
- C005 → 8.05%
- C036 → 11.05%
- C044 → 15.11%
- C024 → 16.66%

Most cadets in the lower progress category also had high remaining flight hours, indicating that they may require additional scheduling support to complete training on time.

---

# Remaining flight hours

Cadets enrolled in IR and CPL programs generally showed higher remaining hour requirements due to the larger training targets associated with those courses.

The highest remaining hour values were observed for:
- C005 → 183.9 hours
- C036 → 177.9 hours
- C004 → 167.0 hours

Cadets with lower remaining hours were mostly found in the final stage of training progression.

Examples:
- C045 → 5.9 hours remaining
- C019 → 7.0 hours remaining
- C037 → 7.1 hours remaining

---

# Average flying rate analysis

Average flying rate was calculated using total flown hours and training duration.

Some cadets showed very low flying rates over long enrollment durations:
- C024 → 1.04
- C025 → 1.03
- C015 → 1.23
- C042 → 1.09

This indicates slow operational progression despite long training periods.

A few cadets showed unusually high flying rates because of very recent enrollment dates combined with rapid flying activity. These values may require operational verification.

Examples:
- C007
- C011
- C049

# Estimated completion risk

Completion risk was estimated using:
- training progress percentage
- flying rate
- study performance
- outstanding payments
- operational disruptions

Cadets classified under high completion risk included:
- C005
- C015
- C024
- C025
- C039
- C044

Common patterns observed among high-risk cadets:
- very low flying progress
- low TOGA study progress
- high outstanding payment amounts
- low operational activity

Example observation:

C005 is currently at 8.05% training completion with 183.9 remaining flight hours and low study progress. The cadet also has a high outstanding payment amount, increasing the probability of delayed course completion.

---

# Cadets at risk of delay

A large number of cadets were classified under medium risk due to moderate progress levels and lower flying rates.

Several PPL cadets showed slow progression despite longer enrollment durations. Some IR cadets also showed moderate operational progress but weaker academic performance.

Weather-related disruptions were relatively limited in the dataset, but repeated cancellations still contributed to medium-risk classification for some cadets.

---

# Lesson type delay and cancellation analysis

Circuit and Solo lessons recorded the highest average delays at approximately 19.7 minutes.

Instrument lessons recorded the highest cancellation count with 5 cancellations.

| lesson type | observation |
|-------------|-------------|
| Circuit | highest average delay |
| Solo | high average delay |
| Instrument | highest cancellations |
| General Handling | lowest average delay |

This suggests that Instrument training sessions may face higher operational dependency and scheduling complexity.

Low sortie participation may indicate:

---

# Conclusion

The analysis identified noticeable differences in cadet training progression across the academy.

While some cadets are progressing steadily toward completion, others show signs of operational and academic delay. The major contributing factors observed were:
- low flying activity
- high remaining flight hours
- weaker TOGA study performance
- outstanding payment balances
- repeated cancellations

The analysis also indicates that instructor workload balancing and lesson scheduling efficiency can influence cadet progression rates. Improving sortie allocation consistency and monitoring high-risk cadets early may help reduce future training delays.
