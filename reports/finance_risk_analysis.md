# Finance risk analysis

## Overview

This report summarizes the financial risk analysis conducted using cadet payment records and training progress information. The objective was to identify cadets with higher financial risk, understand how payment behavior may affect training continuity, and detect possible revenue leakage areas.

---

# Outstanding amount analysis

Outstanding balances varied significantly across cadets.

The highest outstanding values were observed for:

- C004 → ₹422,166
- C045 → ₹403,190
- C056 → ₹387,854
- C038 → ₹370,815
- C055 → ₹364,827

Some cadets maintained relatively lower outstanding balances:

- C048 → ₹1,603
- C006 → ₹3,304
- C054 → ₹10,196

The large variation indicates differences in payment completion status across the cadet population.

---

# Relationship between payment and training continuity

The comparison between payment status and training progression showed several cases where high outstanding balances existed alongside lower training progress.

Examples:

- C005 → ₹280,345 outstanding with 8.05% training progress
- C004 → ₹422,166 outstanding with 16.50% training progress
- C044 → ₹331,515 outstanding with 15.11% training progress

This pattern suggests that financial factors may potentially affect continuity of training activities.

At the same time, a few cadets maintained strong training progress despite high outstanding amounts.

Examples:

- C045 → 86.88% training progress
- C055 → 84.66% training progress
- C058 → 74.00% training progress

This indicates that payment status alone may not fully explain training performance.

# Revenue leakage indicators

Revenue leakage indicators were identified by checking larger outstanding amounts together with long periods since the previous payment.

Examples identified:

- C045 → ₹403,190 outstanding with 81 days since last payment
- C058 → ₹308,153 outstanding with 83 days since last payment
- C033 → ₹292,481 outstanding with 80 days since last payment
- C015 → ₹294,895 outstanding with 61 days since last payment

These records may require follow-up from the finance team.

---

# Key observations

- High-risk payment cases mostly involved large outstanding balances combined with older payment dates.
- Some cadets showed weak payment completion together with lower training progress percentages.
- A few cadets maintained good training performance despite financial risk indicators.
- Revenue leakage indicators suggest possible follow-up opportunities for pending collections.

---

# conclusion

The financial analysis identified a group of cadets with elevated payment-related risk. Large outstanding amounts and delayed payment activity appeared as the major contributors to financial risk.

Early identification of these cases may help reduce possible disruptions in training continuity and improve payment recovery tracking. Continuous monitoring of high-risk records can support both operational planning and financial stability.
